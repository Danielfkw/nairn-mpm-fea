Using Damage Initiation Laws

Some materials allow the use of damage initiation laws. The possible laws are described fully on the OSUPDocs Wiki. This section briefly lists the properties. Damage initiation law properties are assigned within a material definition that uses the law:

<Material Type='50' Name='Isotropic Softening'>
         ⋮
     <Initiation>(Law_Name or number)</Initiation>
     (Law properties)
</Material>

where the Initiation command selects the law (using the law name or number from the table below). The law selection is followed by commands to set any properties specific to that law. Available softening materials can use damage initiation laws.

The following table lists the available damage initiation laws. Click any one to see the law and the properties needed for that law.

NameNumberDescription
MaxPrinciple 1 Maximum principle stress or maximum shear stress

Maximum Principle Stress (number=1)

The damage initiates when either the maximum principle stress exceeds the tensile strength or the maximum shear stress exceeds the shear strength. The input properties are:

  • sigmac - the tensile strength (in pressure units). The default is very large number which means the material will not fail in tension.
  • tauc - the shear strength (in pressure units). The default is very large number which means the material will not fail in shear.
